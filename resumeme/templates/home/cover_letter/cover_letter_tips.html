Cover Letter Basics

What is a Cover Letter?

A cover letter is a one-page document that you submit as part of your job application, alongside your resume. Its purpose is to introduce yourself and briefly summarize your professional background. A well-written cover letter will provide insight into your qualifications and motivation for the job that might not otherwise be apparent from your resume.

A good cover letter should:

  • Highlight your most relevant qualifications for the job
  • Explain why you're interested in the position and organization
  • Show how your skills and experience make you a good fit
  • Demonstrate your writing and communication skills
  • Convey your enthusiasm and personality

When to Include a Cover Letter

You should always include a cover letter unless the job posting specifically states not to. Even if it's optional, a well-crafted cover letter can help you stand out from other candidates and demonstrate your interest in the position.

The Three Main Types of Cover Letters

1. Application Cover Letter

The most common type, written in response to a specific job opening. It addresses the requirements in the job posting and highlights your relevant qualifications.

2. Prospecting Cover Letter

Sent to a company you're interested in that hasn't advertised any openings. It expresses your interest in the organization and inquires about potential opportunities that match your skills.

3. Networking Cover Letter

Used when someone in your network has referred you to a position or when you're requesting information, assistance, or advice regarding your career path.

Cover Letter vs. Resume: Key Differences

Cover Letter Resume
Narrative format with complete sentences Concise bullet points and phrases
Explains your interest in the position/company Lists your qualifications and experience
Expands on selected achievements from your resume Comprehensive overview of your professional background
Shows personality and writing ability Presents facts and credentials objectively
Typically 250-400 words Usually 1-2 pages of content

Cover Letter Structure & Format

Cover Letter Layout

A professional cover letter follows a standard business letter format with proper spacing and margins:

  • Use 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Align text to the left (no justification)
  • Use single spacing with an extra space between paragraphs
  • Choose a professional, readable font (e.g., Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman) in 10-12 point size
  • Keep your cover letter to one page (250-400 words)

File Format and Naming

When submitting your cover letter electronically:

  • Save your cover letter as a PDF to preserve formatting (unless specifically asked for another format)
  • Name your file professionally, including your name and the word "cover letter" (e.g., "John_Smith_Cover_Letter.pdf")
  • If submitting via email, you can either attach your cover letter as a separate document or include it in the body of the email

Pro Tip

Match the style and formatting of your cover letter to your resume for a cohesive, professional application package. This includes using the same header, fonts, and general design elements.

Content Strategies

Compelling Opening Lines

The first paragraph of your cover letter needs to grab the reader's attention and make them want to continue reading. Avoid generic openings like "I am writing to apply for the position..." Instead, try these approaches:

Express genuine enthusiasm:

"As a long-time admirer of XYZ Company's innovative approach to sustainable product design, I was thrilled to discover the Senior Product Developer opening on your team."

Lead with an impressive accomplishment:

"After leading my current marketing team to three industry awards and a 45% increase in client retention last quarter, I am eager to bring my strategic approach to the Marketing Director role at ABC Inc."

Start with a relevant connection:

"When Sarah Johnson mentioned that Acme Solutions was searching for a Project Manager with healthcare industry experience, I knew my background developing medical record systems and leading cross-functional teams would make me an ideal candidate."

Reference recent company news:

"I was impressed by your company's recent announcement about expanding into the Asian market, having just completed a similar international growth initiative that increased global revenue by 27% in my current role."

Showcasing Achievements Effectively

The body of your cover letter should highlight your most relevant achievements and demonstrate how your skills match the job requirements:

Use the PAR Method: Problem, Action, Result

  • Problem: Identify a challenge or opportunity you faced
  • Action: Describe what you did to address it
  • Result: Explain the positive outcome with specific metrics when possible

"When our company's e-commerce conversion rate stalled at 2.3% (Problem), I led a complete redesign of the checkout process, implementing A/B testing and user feedback sessions (Action). This initiative increased conversion rates by 35% and generated an additional $1.2M in revenue within six months (Result)."

Quantify Your Accomplishments

Numbers make your achievements concrete and memorable:

Instead of: "I managed a large team and improved customer satisfaction."

Write: "I led a team of 12 customer service representatives and implemented a new feedback system that improved customer satisfaction scores from 7.8 to 9.2 within three months."

Customization Tips

Researching the Company

Before writing your cover letter, research the company thoroughly to tailor your message effectively:

Key information to research:

  • Company mission and values: Understand what drives the organization
  • Recent news and achievements: Stay updated on company milestones
  • Products or services: Know what they offer and to which markets
  • Company culture: Understand the work environment and team dynamics
  • Industry challenges: Be aware of issues affecting their business

Where to find this information:

  • Company website (About Us, Careers, Blog sections)
  • LinkedIn company page and employee profiles
  • Industry news sites and press releases
  • Annual reports (for public companies)
  • Company reviews on Glassdoor or similar platforms

"I was particularly drawn to Eco Solutions' commitment to reducing plastic waste in consumer packaging, as mentioned in your CEO's recent interview with Sustainability Today. As someone who has led three zero-waste initiatives at my current company, I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to your innovative approach to environmental responsibility."

Addressing the Job Requirements

Analyze the job description carefully and address key requirements in your cover letter:

1. Identify key requirements and qualifications

Make a list of the most important skills, experiences, and qualifications mentioned in the job posting.

2. Match your experiences to requirements

For each key requirement, think of a specific example from your background that demonstrates your capability.

3. Address potential gaps

If you're missing a specific requirement, highlight transferable skills or your ability to learn quickly.

Example: Matching Requirements to Experience

Job Requirement Your Experience (to include in cover letter)
"5+ years of project management experience" "During my 6-year tenure at ABC Corp, I managed 15+ projects with budgets exceeding $500K, consistently delivering on time and under budget."
"Experience with agile methodologies" "I implemented Scrum methodologies for our development team, leading to a 30% reduction in time-to-market for new features."
"Strong communication skills" "As the liaison between technical teams and executive stakeholders, I translated complex technical concepts into clear business terms, ensuring alignment across all levels of the organization."
