Resume Writing Tips

Expert advice to create a resume that gets you noticed and hired

A well-crafted resume is your ticket to landing interviews and ultimately securing your dream job. Based on our analysis of thousands of successful resumes, we've compiled these tips to help you create a document that will impress employers and highlight your qualifications effectively.

General Guidelines

1

Keep it concise

Unless you're applying for an executive or academic position, limit your resume to one page. Recruiters spend an average of just 7 seconds reviewing each resume.

2

Use a clean, professional design

Choose a simple, readable font and maintain consistent formatting throughout. Avoid excessive colors, graphics, or unusual layouts unless you're in a creative field.

3

Tailor for each job application

Customize your resume for each position by emphasizing relevant skills and experiences that match the job description. Use keywords from the job posting to help pass through ATS systems.

Content Optimization

4

Use action verbs

Begin bullet points with strong action verbs like "achieved," "implemented," or "managed" to convey your accomplishments more powerfully.

Example:

✓ Better

"Implemented a new customer service protocol that reduced complaint resolution time by 35%"

✗ Avoid

"Was responsible for customer service and dealing with complaints"

5

Quantify your achievements

Use numbers and percentages to showcase your impact. Specific metrics help employers understand the scope and results of your work.

Example:

✓ Better

"Increased social media followers by 200% in 6 months and boosted engagement by 45%"

✗ Avoid

"Managed company social media accounts and increased followers"

6

Focus on achievements, not just duties

Instead of simply listing job responsibilities, highlight your accomplishments and the value you added to previous employers.

Example:

✓ Better

"Redesigned inventory management process, reducing stockouts by 25% and saving $45,000 annually"

✗ Avoid

"Responsible for managing inventory and ordering supplies"

ATS Optimization

7

Use standard section headings

Use conventional section titles like "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills" to ensure ATS systems properly categorize your information.

8

Include relevant keywords

Incorporate industry-specific terms and skills from the job description to help your resume pass through ATS filters.

9

Avoid complex formatting

Stay away from tables, headers/footers, and text boxes, as many ATS systems cannot properly read this formatting.

Final Touches

10

Proofread carefully

Typos and grammatical errors can immediately disqualify you. Have someone else review your resume before submitting it.

11

Update your contact information

Ensure your email, phone number, and LinkedIn profile are current and professional. Consider adding a link to your portfolio if relevant.

12

Save in the right format

Unless otherwise specified, save and submit your resume as a PDF to preserve formatting across different devices and systems.
