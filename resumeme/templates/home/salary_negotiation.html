Pro Tip: Prepare Your Compensation Range

When researching, identify three numbers:

  • Minimum: The lowest offer you would accept
  • Target: What you believe is fair based on your research
  • Ideal: The best-case scenario that's still within market reality

Having this range prepared gives you flexibility during negotiations.

Benefits Valuation Strategy

When evaluating a full compensation package, consider the monetary value of benefits:

  • Health insurance: $5,000-$20,000+ annually depending on coverage and family size
  • Retirement matching: Often 3-6% of your salary
  • Extra PTO: Calculate your daily rate Ã— number of additional days
  • Remote work: Potential savings on commuting, meals, and professional attire
  • Professional development: Training and certifications can be worth thousands

A lower base salary with exceptional benefits can sometimes exceed the value of a higher salary with minimal benefits.

Remember These Key Principles

Be Confident but Collaborative

Approach negotiation as a problem-solving discussion, not a confrontation. Your goal is a win-win outcome.

Practice Your Delivery

Rehearse your talking points until you can deliver them naturally and confidently.

Listen Actively

Understanding the employer's constraints and priorities can help you find creative solutions.

Get Everything in Writing

Once you've reached an agreement, ensure all details are documented in your offer letter or contract.
